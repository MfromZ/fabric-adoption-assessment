# Mentoring & User Enablement Research

Source: https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/power-bi/guidance/fabric-adoption-roadmap-mentoring-and-user-enablement

## Purpose
Enable users to accomplish as much as possible within governance guardrails. One of the most important COE responsibilities.

## Key Activities
- **Office hours**: Scheduled times for COE experts to help community
- **Co-development projects**: COE works alongside business users to build solutions
- **Best practices reviews**: Review of self-service solutions for quality
- **Training**: Formal and informal learning programs
- **Documentation**: Guidelines, standards, how-to guides
- **Centralized portal**: Single place for resources, training, templates
- **Template files**: Standardized Power BI templates

## Maturity Levels
| Level | State |
|-------|-------|
| 100 | No formal mentoring or enablement, users figure it out alone |
| 200 | Some informal mentoring, ad-hoc training efforts |
| 300 | Initial investments in training, documentation, and resources |
| 400 | Training and resources readily available and actively used by community |
| 500 | Comprehensive programs, continuous improvement, user proficiency tracked |
