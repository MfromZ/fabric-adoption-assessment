# Center of Excellence (COE) Research

Source: https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/power-bi/guidance/fabric-adoption-roadmap-center-of-excellence

## What is a COE?
Internal team of technical and business experts who assist others working with data.

## COE Goals
- Evangelize data-driven culture
- Promote adoption of analytics
- Mentor, guide, and educate users
- Coordinate efforts across organizational boundaries
- Maximize self-service BI benefits while reducing risks
- Reduce technical debt

## COE Structures
- **Centralized**: Single shared services team
- **Unified**: Centralized + embedded team members in business units
- **Federated**: Core team + satellite members from each business unit
- **Decentralized**: Independently managed by business units

## Key Roles
COE Leader, Coach, Trainer, Data Analyst, Data Modeler, Report Creator, Data Engineer, User Support

## Maturity Levels
| Level | State |
|-------|-------|
| 100 | Some form of COE exists but no clarity on goals or expectations |
| 200 | COE in place with charter, goals, scope, staffing, structure, funding established |
| 300 | COE operates with active involvement from all business units (unified/federated) |
| 400 | COE goals align with org goals, well-known and proves value consistently |
| 500 | KPIs/OKRs measure COE effectiveness, continuous improvement is top priority |
